02 — Oath Garden

The Ritual of Gathering

Morning light · Coffee · Natural wine · Community

The day begins here. Hands grinding coffee, the slow pour, ceramics still warm from the kiln. Bread broken over long tables, natural wine as the light turns gold.

This is the ritual of gathering — unhurried, human, alive. Nothing glamorous, everything real: the grain of the wood, the weight of a cup, the conversation between strangers who leave as friends.

The Ritual of Listening

Dim light · Vinyl · Cocktails · Smoke & wood

01

A Slow Becoming

Ten years, told in fragments.

  1. 2017

    The first table

    Six friends, one long plank on trestles, a pot that fed all of them. The word oath said once, half as a joke.

  2. 2018

    Field recordings

    A borrowed recorder. Rain on tin, the night market, a monk's bell at dawn — the first reels of what became the sound library.

  3. 2019

    Clay & fire

    Forty-eight vessels pulled from a backyard kiln. Most cracked. The eight that survived still hold coffee at the Garden.

  4. 2020

    The quiet year

    The world closed its doors. We learned to breathe on purpose. Stillness stopped being absence and became a practice.

  5. 2021

    Long tables, natural wine

    Supper clubs in borrowed courtyards. Strangers seated shoulder to shoulder, leaving as something closer to kin.

  6. 2022

    The listening room

    A valve amplifier, a single turntable, a wall of records. Papersound began as one dim room where sound was the reason.

  7. 2023

    Breath & concrete

    A bare floor, linen, slow light. The first morning sessions — the body returning to itself. Oath Studio, in seed.

  8. 2024

    A house is drawn

    1,400 sketches. Three programmes — food, sound, movement — folded into one building under one roof in Vientiane.

  9. 2025

    Ground broken

    Ban Wat Chan. Dust, rebar, and the smell of cut timber. The drawings became walls you could put a hand against.

  10. 2026

    The doors open

    A House of Rituals, at last. Everything before this was rehearsal.

03

A Note, Unsent for Years

To whoever finds this room —

Oath was never meant to be a business. It was a way of staying close to people in a city learning to rush. We cooked because it gathered us. We collected records because a song can hold a room still. We breathed, deliberately, because the body forgets how.

For nine years it had no address. It lived in courtyards and borrowed kitchens, on cassette reels and in a thousand drawings. What you walk into in 2026 is only the building. The house itself is older, and you are already inside it.

Stay late. Eat slowly. Let the room hold you.

— The founders, Vientiane
